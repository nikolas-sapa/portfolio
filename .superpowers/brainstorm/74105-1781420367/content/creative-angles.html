Out-of-the-Box Plays

Not generic advice — moves that fit you specifically

Lead Magnet — Kill the PDF
A

Build an actual micro-tool

"Paste your website → get a custom AI automation roadmap." Powered by Claude, automated. Free, instant, shareable. Email captured to unlock the full report. This is what Pieter Levels would do — he'd never make a PDF. He'd ship a tool. The tool IS the lead magnet AND demonstrates you can build.

B

Personalized 2-min Loom for every lead

"Fill out this form → Nikolas records a 2-min breakdown of what he'd build for your business." Semi-automated: form → Claude generates brief → you record → they get it in 24h. Nobody does this. It's memorable, it's personal, it shows confidence. First 20 clients come from this alone.

C

Live build log on the website

Homepage has a terminal window showing what you're building RIGHT NOW. Current project, last commit, hours in. Not a portfolio — a workspace in progress. People bookmark it. Journalists screenshot it. It's the most honest signal of capability you can send.

X Strategy — Not "Do More Replies"
A

The Target 20

Pick 20 founders you want to know you. Not 500. 20. Engage with ONLY them for 90 days — deeply, genuinely, add value every time. Those 20 become your connectors. One reply from the right person is worth 1000 from strangers. Concentrated relationships beat spray-and-pray.

B

"I'll build this for you" posts

Every week: pick a well-known founder/product. Publicly build them an AI tool — an automation, an agent, an analysis. Tag them. Ship it. Sometimes they respond. Always their audience sees it. Builds portfolio in public, shows capability without claiming it. The age + output is the hook.

C

Own the Claude Code space

Claude Code is hot and growing. Very few people document what's actually possible with it (not the hype — the real workflows, the real setups). Be THE person for this. One well-placed thread becomes the reference link people share. You're already living it — just document it publicly.

IG — Formats That Break Through
A

Build it live in one video

Start a video with "I'm going to build [thing] in the next 20 minutes." Film the whole thing. Real-time. Show what breaks. Ship it at the end. This is insane proof of capability and nobody in your space does it. The 16-year-old building production AI live on camera is its own story.

B

Public roast format

"Show me your AI setup / website / workflow and I'll roast it." Pick a commenter every week. Build or fix something for them on camera. Creates engagement, social proof, demonstrates taste. Directly connected to Creator Roast — your product and your content become the same thing.

C

The anti-portfolio

Document projects that died. Real numbers, real reasons. This is the rarest content because nobody admits failure with specifics. Builds more trust than 10 success posts. The brand DNA already says "honesty" — this is what that actually looks like in practice.

Newsletter — Not Another Newsletter
A

Every issue ships with a real artifact

Not just words. Every issue includes one usable thing: a Claude skill file, a workflow diagram, an actual prompt, a script. Readers forward it because they can immediately use it. The newsletter becomes known as the one that ships something every week.

B

The AI Transparency Report

Monthly: "Every AI tool I used, for what, time saved, cost, honest verdict." No one does this with real numbers. Extremely shareable. Makes you the person to follow if you want to know what AI actually does in a real builder's workflow — not what the hype says.

C

The raw build log

Every issue: what shipped, what died, what surprised you. Numbers. No polish. Late Sunday night. The subject line is always the week's most honest sentence. Readers feel like they're inside the process. Competes on authenticity, not production value.
